# Presentation Guide

This split is set up for about 22 to 24 minutes total, which leaves a little buffer for transitions and one or two audience questions.

The compiled deck has 24 pages.

## Suggested split

### Vineet Reddy

Slides 1 to 8  
Target time: about 7 minutes

Cover:
- title and project question
- why Mars power is hard
- what technologies we compare
- the three project questions
- data sources
- workflow
- colony demand setup
- reliability model

### Samuel Heinz

Slides 9 to 16  
Target time: about 7 to 8 minutes

Cover:
- cost and feasibility model
- forecast side analysis
- resource view
- cost view
- what the cost figure means
- baseline reliability
- reliability by scenario

### Nicholas Fitzpatrick

Slides 17 to 24  
Target time: about 7 to 8 minutes

Cover:
- key reliability numbers
- scaling pressure
- sensitivity checks
- forecast summary
- answers to the three questions
- limits and main takeaways

## Pacing notes

- Try to spend about 45 to 60 seconds on the setup slides.
- Spend closer to 75 to 90 seconds on the big figure slides.
- Do not read the tables line by line, just point out the pattern and one or two anchor numbers.
- The last speaker should leave about 1 minute for the final takeaway slide and transition into questions.
